About ANDHII

The Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics Health Informatics Infrastructure (ANDHII®) enables RDNs to track nutrition care outcomes and advance evidence-based nutrition practice research. ANDHII is a secure online data collection tool accessible from any web or mobile connected device. Using familiar nutrition terminology (NCPT) and a format that makes it easy to integrate into practice workflow, de-identified case data is recorded. The knowledge gained through the collection and analysis of information in ANDHII will add to the evidence base for nutrition practice and help ensure high-quality patient care. Aggregated data can be used to track patient outcomes and understand nutrition care practice patterns.

CDR-credentialed practitioners and Academy members, including students, can all register to use ANDHII.

You can read about the Electronic Nutrition Care Process Terminology (eNCPT) here.

Participating in ANDHII at Your Facility

ANDHII has been designed to work within the regulations and guidance issued by the Office for Civil Rights and Office for Human Research Protections so that the data collected would not be Protected Health Information and the collection process would not constitute Human Subjects Research. As a result, using ANDHII in accordance with regulations and its End User License Agreement for the collection of de-identified data regarding routine care would not require HIPAA Authorizations, Business Associate Agreements, or local Institutional Review.

For use of ANDHII concerning interoperability with EHR and the involvement of PHI, HIPAA compliance issues will apply. Please inquire with the ANDHII team by emailing us at andhii [at] eatright [dot] org

ANDHII FAQs

How do I register for an account?

You can register for an account by navigating to the ANDHII Self-Registration website. Enter your cdrnet.org or eatright.org username and password and click ‘submit’. You will then receive a message to the e-mail you have associated with cdrnet.org or eatright.org asking to verify your account. Click the link in the e-mail and continue to follow the instructions to complete your account registration. This link will be active for 48 hours. If you receive an error message stating the link is inactive, reach out to andhii [at] eatright [dot] org to request a new link to be sent.

I lost my password. How do I recover my password?

You can change your password by selecting ‘Forgot Password?’ on ANDHII’s log-in page. Enter the email address that is associated with your ANDHII account (i.e. the e-mail address you use to login to cdrnet.org or eatright.org) and click ‘Reset Password’. You will then receive a message to this e-mail asking to verify your account. Click the link in the e-mail and continue to follow the instructions to update your password. This link will be active for 48 hours. If you receive an error message stating the link is inactive, reach out to andhii [at] eatright [dot] org to request a new link to be sent.

I lost my signature phrase. How do I recover my signature phrase?

To change your signature phrase, log into ANDHII and navigate to the ‘Profile’ tab. In the right-hand corner, click ‘Forgot Signature Phrase?’. You will then receive a message to the e-mail you have associated with your ANDHII account (i.e. the email address you use to login to cdrnet.org or eatright.org). Click the link in the e-mail and continue to follow the instructions to update your signature phrase. This link will be active for 48 hours. If you receive an error message stating the link is inactive, reach out to andhii [at] eatright [dot] org to request a new link to be sent.

How do I enter a patient?

Log into ANDHII and navigate to the ‘Patients’ tab. Click the green ‘Add patient’ button. A box will appear asking you to select a project for the patient. If you would like to enter a practice or test patient, select Education and Practice. If you would like to enter real patient data into the National Quality Improvement registry, select National Quality Improvement Pool. If you are participating in a special project, select the project’s name from the drop-down. Click ‘Add Patient to This Project’. Continue to follow the prompts to create a new patient.

Is there a way for me to practice using ANDHII?

You can enter practice information into the ‘Education and Practice’ project. I hope you were able to access the Smart Visit Tutorials on the Help & Training Center (www.andhii.org/info), which explains how to use ANDHII.

How do I find a patient to enter a follow-up note?

Log into ANDHII and navigate to the ‘Patients’ tab. Start typing the patient’s ANDHII re-identification code into the ‘search’ bar at the top right of the patient list. If you’re unable to find the patient, try entering just the first few letters or numbers of the code. If there is a zero (0) in the code, this may instead be the letter ‘O’ or the opposite – it’s often difficult to determine if the code includes a zero or a letter ‘O’ by sight. Alternatively, you can scroll through the list of patients by navigating to the next list page at the bottom right of the list. Once you locate the patient, you can click ‘New Entry’ to enter a follow-up visit.

How do I find an incomplete entry?

Log into ANDHII and navigate to the ‘Patients’ tab. Using the ‘search’ bar at the top right of the patient list, enter the patient’s ANDHII re-identification code. Under ‘Incomplete Entry’, you will see a list of dates of incomplete entries. Click on the date to open the incomplete entry. Alternatively, you can select ‘View Details’, which will open the patient’s page in ANDHII. At the top of the page will be a list of ‘Previous Visit Entries’. If there are any finalized or incomplete entries for the patient, the dates of the entries will be listed in this list. Incomplete entries are marked with a red asterisk (*). Click on the date to open the incomplete entry.

How do I create a PDF of my visit entry?

After finalizing each entry, you will be taken to the “Entry Summary Report” page, which will translates the content of the entry into a text format that you can export. You can also navigate to the “Reports” tab in your ANDHII interface. Select ‘Individual Visit Summary’. Select the patient’s ANDHII re-identification code from the ‘Patient’ drop-down and visit entry date from the ‘Entry’ drop-down. Click ‘Export PDF’.

Dietetics Outcomes Registry & Research

RDNs and NDTRs enter data directly into ANDHII and can submit the case into the Dietetics Outcomes Registry (DOR). The powerful and flexible database allows direct-care practitioners in a variety of practice settings (from acute care and private practice to public health nutrition) to enter information about nutrition assessments, diagnosis, interventions, monitoring, evaluation and outcomes. Aggregated, anonymous data from ANDHII use and the DOR supports outcomes research, quality improvement projects and comparative effectiveness research.

Nutrition researchers can leverage ANDHII’s customizable, nutrition-centric data collection platform for experimental research and investigations.
