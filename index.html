About

My story

I am an advocate for intelligent use of technology to streamline the workflow and make life easier. I value human diversity-- each person has their own story to tell and their own experiences to bring to bear. Additionally, I am passionate about science, community-building, and sustainability.

I have been in love with technology since I got my first computer when I was 8 years old. Since then, I have developed skills in content creation, HTML/CSS, graphic design for print and digital use, photography, database management, statistical analysis, and general software and hardware troubleshooting. I built my first computer as a junior in high school at the Mississippi School for Mathematics and Science. Since then, I have worked in computer sales, computer repair, and administered technical support in an academic/educational setting and for a non-profit organization.

My educational background is in biological science, particularly conservation and ecology. I have performed field research with birds, bats, and plants. I earned a B.Sc. in ecology from University of Georgia and an M.Sc. in biological science from University of Mississippi. My Master's thesis work involved the threatened Rafinesque's big-eared bat (Corynorhinus rafinesquii). I have taught biology labs at the University of Mississippi, and I currently teach distance-learning/virtual biology at Northeast Mississippi Community College.

I previously worked as a support technician at the Computer Services Department at Northeast Mississippi Community College and most recently as the IT Manager at the Family Resource Center of North Mississippi, an educational and human services non-profit organization.

In early 2019, I made a major life change and moved to the Pacific Northwest. In my spare time, I enjoy hiking with my dogs, craft beer, vegan cooking, [amateur] weightlifting, college football, playing soccer (the original football), computer games, and learning about space. My favorite music genres are heavy metal, synthwave, and blues.
