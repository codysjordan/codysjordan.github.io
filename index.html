About

My Story

I am a advocate for intelligent use of technology to streamline the workflow and make life easier. Additionally, I am passionate about conservation science, ecological restoration and preservation, and sustainability.

I have been in love with technology since I got my first computer when I was 8 years old. Since then, I have developed skills in content creation, HTML/CSS, graphic design for print and digital use, photography, database management, statistical analysis, and general software and hardware troubleshooting. I built my first computer as a junior in high school at the Mississippi School for Mathematics and Science. Since then, I have worked in computer sales, computer repair, and provided technical support in an educational setting and for non-profit organizations.

My educational background is in biological science, particularly conservation and ecology. I have performed field research with birds, bats, and plants. I earned a B.Sc. in ecology from University of Georgia and an M.Sc. in biological science from University of Mississippi My Master's thesis work involved the threatened Rafinesque's big-eared bat (Corynorhinus rafinesquii). I have taught biology at the University of Mississippi and I currently teach distance learning biology courses at Northeast Mississippi Community College.
